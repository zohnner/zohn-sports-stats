
## **Executive Summary**

On April 9, 2026, Major League Baseball and Nike unveiled the second wave of City Connect uniform designs for eight teams: the **Atlanta Braves, Baltimore Orioles, Cincinnati Reds, Kansas City Royals, Milwaukee Brewers, Pittsburgh Pirates, San Diego Padres, and Texas Rangers**. The following report details each team's uniform palette, including primary base colors and accent shades.

## 1. Atlanta Braves

The Atlanta Braves' design is a modernized callback to the club's beloved 1980s "Superstation" era uniforms.

### Color Palette

| Color Name | Description | HEX Code |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Powder Blue** | A brighter, modern take on the fan-favorite 80s powder blue. This is the primary base color of the uniform. | `#7CB9E8` |
| **White** | Used for the primary lettering of the "Atlanta" script, creating a classic contrast against the powder blue. | `#FFFFFF` |
| **Red** | Used for the outline of the white lettering and the piping along the jersey and pants, tying in the team's classic colors. | `#CE1141` |
| **Navy** | Used for player numerals on the front and player names on the back of the jersey. | `#13274F` |

- **Concept & Inspiration:** The design is a "modernized callback to the Club's beloved 1980s uniforms" featuring a brighter powder blue jersey, updated "Atlanta" script logo, red piping, and an 'ATL' block letter sleeve patch.
- **Cap & Pants:** The ensemble is paired with a powder blue cap featuring a blue bill, red piping, and an updated Cooperstown "a" logo, along with white pants featuring prominent red and blue piping.
- **On-Field Debut:** Friday, April 10, 2026.

## Implementation Notes for Web Design

1.  **Color Usage:**
    - **Braves**: The powder blue (`#7CB9E8`) is vibrant and works well as a primary background color, ideally paired with white text or navy accents for contrast.
    - **Orioles**: The cream (`#F5F5DC`) and Camden green (`#005A3C`) combination is unique and sophisticated, suitable for vintage or classic themed websites.
    - **Reds**: The bold red (`#C6011F`) demands attention. It is best used as an accent color or paired with very light grays and whites to prevent overwhelming the design.
    - **Royals**: The white base (`#FFFFFF`) with a fuchsia-to-blue gradient (`#FF00AF` to `#004687`) is highly modern and dynamic, ideal for creating energetic call-to-action buttons or hero sections.
    - **Brewers**: The Great Lakes blue (`#236B8E`) and cream (`#F5F5DC`) palette provides a calm, natural feel that can be extended with sunset gradient elements for a modern, outdoorsy aesthetic.
    - **Pirates**: The matte black (`#27251F`) and gold (`#FDB827`) scheme is sleek and powerful, perfect for entertainment, gaming, or sports websites where a bold, premium feel is desired.
    - **Padres**: The six-color palette is complex. Obsidian (`#1C1F2A`) or Bone (`#F3EFE0`) can serve as the dominant neutral, while Marigold (`#FCA120`), Aqua (`#00B5B8`), Fireberry (`#C82B4F`), and Padres Gold (`#F9B01D`) can be used as vibrant, contrasting accents.
    - **Rangers**: The Cochineal red (`#9D2024`) is a striking, unique shade of red. It works well for establishing a strong visual impact when paired with cream (`#F5F5DC`) to soften the overall palette.

2.  **Gradients as Primary Elements:**
    The designs from the **Kansas City Royals** and **Milwaukee Brewers** heavily feature watercolor-style gradients. These can be implemented as a key visual element rather than a traditional solid background. Transparent gradients overlaid on hero images could be an effective way to incorporate the fluid, water-inspired themes of both teams.

3.  **Accessibility Consideration:**
    Ensure that when using the **Braves'** powder blue or the **Royals'** white base with vibrant accent colors, there is sufficient contrast for text. The **Reds'** red and the **Padres'** obsidian will likely require white or very light-colored text for accessibility.
